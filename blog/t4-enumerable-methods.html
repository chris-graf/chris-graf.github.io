How to Surive Your
Island Adventure:
group_by

2015 August 02

Picture this: you're stranded on a tropical island about 500 miles off the Polynesian coast. Your stores of rainwater are running low, and you've just raided the beach's last cache of delicious turtle eggs. Behind you lies the Pacific Ocean, majestic and terrifying in its indifference to your plight. Before you lies a viny thicket of palms, echoing with the chirps of a hundred thousand beetles and the roar of at least one island jaguar. Your only tools are a hollowed out coconut, a crude driftwood spear, and a solar-powered Macbook Air with a wideband satellite uplink. Your task: take an list of the fearsome beasts you will have to face in the jungle, and group them based on their threat level.

You have the following list of dangers:
jungle_foes = ["scarlet baboon", "island jaguar", "angry turtle", "Polynesian kangaroo spider", "delicious blue-spotted tree frog", "poisonous indigo-spotted tree frog", "climate change"]

And the following function that evaluates their threat level:
def assess_threat(creature)
if (creature.include? "poisonous") || (creature.include? "jaguar")
return "high"
elsif (creature.include? "spider") || (creature.include? "climate")
return "medium"
else
return "low"
end
end

How do you prepare yourself for the journey ahead? You know you could declare a new hash, iterate over the list with each passing assess_threat in a block, and push each item to an appropriate value array using its assess_threat output as the key. But that would take several lines of code, and in this perilous paradise you must cherish every calorie. What if you could accomplish all that in a single line?
jungle_foes.group_by { |item| assess_threat(item) }
=> {"high"=>["island jaguar", "poisonous indigo-spotted tree frog"], "medium"=>["Polynesian kangaroo spider", "climate change"], "low"=>["scarlet baboon", "angry turtle", "delicious blue-spotted tree frog"] }

Now you're ready to forage for sustenance. Remember the power of group_by the next time you need to group an enumerable into a hash based on the result of a block.
